The Midnight Zone

Sunlight is unable to reach this far into the ocean, causing this zone to be completely dark. Any light source that exists from here on down, comes from cool features that animals have themselves.

3,280-13,125 Feet

Starfish

"Oh hey, it's Patrick!" Just kidding. Starfish actually exist in real life. You may see a starfish or two laying on the shore, but they are most commonly found on the deep seafloor, around 6000 feet. Surprisingly, starfish can survive with no brain. They may be difficult to spot, but starfish possess hundreds of microscopic feet, which help them cling to their environment. Starfish have the incredible ability to regenerate their limbs if one or two are dismembered or chopped off.

Viperfish

Viperfish live in the deeper parts of the ocean where the water is so dark they are practically invisible to other fish, making them one of the fiercest predators in the deep seas. Photophores along the side of their bodies produce light, and can be flashed on and off to attract prey. A “fishing lure” at the top of their heads, similar to that of an anglerfish, also comes to benefit in this aspect of survival. . Viperfish have large, fang-like teeth that can arch over the prey's head, nearly reaching its eyes.During the day, they hunt at around 5,000 feet (1,500 meters) below the ocean surface where almost no light can penetrate through the water. As night falls, they venture up to around 2,000 feet (600 meters) below the surface in search of food at shallower depths, where it is often more plentiful.

Vampire Squid

Living 3,000 feet below sea level, these creatures employ their bioluminescent organs and favorable oxygen metabolism to survive the parts of the ocean where oxygen levels are extremely low. They are considered both a squid and an octopus, but it can also be argued that they are neither of the sort. The name “vampire squid” originates from the creature’s dark skin, which connects its limbs to form a cape-like structure similar to a vampire's shawl. Each one of their arms has large spines running along the back, that are arranged into two rows. In comparison to their body, vampire squid have some of the largest eyes, which can be either red or blue depending on their environment.
