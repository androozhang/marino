The Midnight Zone

Sunlight is unable to reach this far into the ocean, causing this zone to be completely dark.
Any light source that exists from here on down, comes from cool features that animals have themselves.

3,280 to 13,125 Feet

Starfish

""Oh hey, it's Patrick!" Just kidding. Starfish actually exist in real life. Surprisingly, they have no brain, which perfectly describes why Spongebob's pink friend acts the way he does. It might be hard to see, but starfish have hundreds of feet, which helps them cling to their environment. Chopping off a leg or two means nothing to starfishes, since they can regenerate their arms in just months.

Viperfish

Viperfish live in deep parts of the ocean where it is so dark that they are invisible to other fish, making them one of the most fierce predators living in the deep ocean. They use photophores along the side of their bodies to help hide their body from other fish swimming beneath them. A lot like the anglerfish, they use a "fishing lure" on top of their head to attract prey. These fish have large fang-like teeth that their teeth can curve over the prey's head and almost to their eyes when their mouth is closed. During the day, they hunt prey around 5,000 feet (1,500 meters) below the surface where almost no light can penetrate through the water. As it turns to night, they move up to around 2,000 feet (600 meters) to search for food where it is more plentiful at shallower depths.

Vampire Squid

Living below 3,000 feet below sea level, these creatures use bioluminescent organs and oxygen metabolism to survive the parts of water that have extremely low levels of oxygen. They are both a squid and an octopus but at the same time, they are neither of them. The name vampire squid comes from its dark color and the skin that connects all of its arms forming a cape-like structure like a vampire's cape. Each one of their eight arms has spines that are arranged into two rows. They have one of the largest eyes in comparison to the size of their body. Their eyes are either red or blue depending on the light that they are on.
