The Sunlight Zone

Also known as the euphotic zone, this area of the ocean contains many of the most popular marine animals. This zone receives enough sunlight for plants to perform photosynthesis.

0-700 Feet

Dolphin

Dolphins are widely known for their intelligence and playfulness around humans. They are mammals, meaning they breathe oxygen through a blowhole at the top of their heads. In terms of social behaviour, dolphins are very gregarious, travelling in groups called "pods", which contain hundreds of individuals. They hunt and prey on other marine wildlife by means of a sonar system known as echolocation.

Jellyfish

Jellyfish are invertebrates, which indicates the absence of a backbone in their skeletal makeup. Thin tentacles containing tiny stinging cells are characteristic to these Cnidarians, and help them prey on small plants and other marine organisms for sustenance. Jellyfish propel themselves through the water by contracting and relaxing a ring of muscles around their bodies, expelling water to push them forward. Despite the deadly nature of their tentacles, they are typically docile creatures unless provoked.

Turtle

Different species of sea turtles vary in appearance and behavior. Some may have adopted a more herbivorous diet based on underwater vegetation such as seagrass. By keeping the seagrass at a suitable length, sea turtles prevent it from overgrowing and potentially bringing harm to the ecosystem. Other sea turtles may enjoy feasting on jellyfish populations, keeping the number of jellyfish at a sustainable capacity and filling a biological role very similar to the one of dolphins. Unfortunately, around a thousand sea turtles die every year by ingesting plastic, believing it to be jellyfish due to its deceiving appearance.Baby sea turtles' sex is oftentimesdictated by the temperature. Depending on where they're laid, warmer nests generally lead to the birth of females and cooler ones lead to the birth of males.
