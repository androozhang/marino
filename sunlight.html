The Sunlight Zone

Also known as the euphotic zone, this area of the ocean contains many of the most popular marine animals.
This zone receives enough sunlight for plants to perform photosynthesis.

0-700 Feet

Dolphin

Dolphins are typically known for their intelligence and their playfulness around humans. They can breathe through a blowhole on the top of their head. They use a sonar system called echolocation to hunt and prey on marine animals. Dolphins are very social and live in groups called "pods", which contain hundreds of individuals.

Jellyfish

Jellyfish are invertebrates, which means they have no backbones. Their thin tentacles have tiny stinging cells that help them prey on their food. They move through the ocean by squirting water out of their mouths, propelling them forward. Although their stings can be painful, they don't harm humans unless provoked.

Turtle

Some sea turtles have more of a plant-based diet based on underwater vegetation such as seagrass. By keeping the seagrass at a short length, sea turtles prevent it from harming other marine animals. Other sea turtles, however, enjoy feasting on jellyfish to keeps their populations in check, very similar to dolphins. Plastic is a sea turtle's worst nightmare since it looks like jellyfish when floating around in the water. This is also a big reason why many sea turtles die from ingesting plastic. Baby sea turtles' sex is drastically dictated by the temperature. Depending on where they're laid, warmer nests generally lead to the birth of females and cooler ones lead to the birth of males.
