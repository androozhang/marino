The Sunlight Zone

Also known as the euphotic zone, this area of the ocean contains many of the most popular marine animals.
This zone receives enough sunlight for plants to perform photosynthesis.

0-700 Feet

Dolphin

Dolphins are usually known for their intelligence and their playfulness around humans. They can breathe through a blowhole on the top of their head. They use a form of sonar called echolocation to hunt and prey on marine animals. Dolphins are very social marine animals that live in groups called "pods", which generally contain hundreds of individuals.

Jellyfish

Jellyfish are invertebrates, meaning they have no backbones. They have thin tentacles that have tiny stinging cells to help them prey on their food. They move through the ocean by squirting water out of their mouths, propelling them forward. Although their stings could be painful, they don't harm humans unless bothered.

Turtle

Some sea turtles have more of a plant-based diet eating seagrass. By keeping the seagrass short it prevents them from harming other marine animals when it grows taller. Other sea turtles however enjoy feasting on jellyfish which keeps their populations in check, very similar to dolphins. Plastic is one of sea turtles' worst nightmares since they look like jellyfish when it's floating around in the water. This is also a big reason why many sea turtles die from ingesting plastic. Baby sea turtles' sex is drastically dictated by the temperature. Depending on where they're laid, warmer nests generally lead to females and cooler ones lead to males.
