The Abyss

Almost at freezing temperature, this area contains some of the ocean's scariest creatures.

13,125-19,690 Feet

Anglerfish

The most distinctive feature about anglerfish, worn only by females, is a piece of dorsal spine that comes out above their mouths. It looks like a glowing bait on a fishing line and they wave it around to attract their prey. Most of them are colored to blend into the background of the sea. These creatures have a stout body, a large head, and a ginormous mouth. Unfortunately for males, their sole purpose in life is to find a female to mate with. Once the male finds a partner he will latch onto her with his teeth and eventually his flesh will become a part of hers.

Dumbo Octopus

The Dumbo octopus was named after the Disney Character "Dumbo the elephant", who was famous for its big ears that allowed him to fly. Dumbo octopus uses their ear-like fins to propel them through the water and steer using its webbed arms. Unlike other octopuses, the Dumbo octopus doesn't have an ink sac because it doesn't or rarely encounters predators so it has no use for it. Living at least 13,100 feet (4,000m) below the ocean surface, making it the deepest living octopus species known to mankind. They feed on snails, worms, and other creatures that they hover over on the ocean floor.

Gulper Eel

The gulper eel, also known as pelican eel, has an unusually large mouth and can open their mouths to swallow much larger prey. Their lower haw is very similar to a pelican, hence the name pelican eel. They may have a large mouth and stomach but the teeth are fairly small. Their main source of food comes from crustaceans, which are like shrimp. crab and a lobster mixed together. Unlike other fish species, the gulper eel doesn't have swim bladders, scales, and pelvic fins. The long tail is the only way the fish is able to move around underwater. Living as deep as 9,800 feet below water, their only possible predator is the lancetfish.
