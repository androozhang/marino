Shark

Common Octopus

Octopuses are marine creatures famous for their eight long arms, rounded body, and ability to camouflage. If threatened, they will discharge inky fluids from their bodies, darkening the water around them to confuse their predators. Octopuses are invertebrates without all but a skull for its skeletal structure, which improves their mobility and makes them fantastic escapists.

Whale

Although whales spend most of their time underwater feeding and mating, they come up to the surface from time to time to breathe. Whales voyage across oceans as one of the world's largest animals. Like dolphins, whales are mammals, meaning they breathe through lungs rather than gills. They rise to the ocean surface, exhaling water vapor out of their blowholes in exchange for oxygen.
