Shark

Sharks are known for being fast swimmers and having to constantly move around to survive. Unlike human skeletons, their skeletons are made out of cartilage instead of actual bones. Surprisingly enough, even though they don't have bones, they can still fossilize. They have amazing eyesight and they're able to see well in dark-lighted areas with night vision. Over the years, researchers have found a unique way to measure the shark's age. They count the age of the shark by looking at the rings on their vertebrae.

Common Octopus

Octopuses are marine creatures famous for their eight long arms, rounded body, and being able to camouflage. If threatened, they will shoot out inky fluids from their body darkening the water around them confusing the predators. Octopuses are invertebrates and don't have bones, which makes them fantastic escapists.

Whale

Known for their large size, whales swim across oceans as one of the world's largest animals. Although whales swim in the ocean, they breathe with their lungs instead of gills. They rise to the ocean surface and blow water vapors out of their blowholes. Their blowholes are located right on top of their body and are used for breathing.
